Step 1 · Open
Start on their vision, not yours
›

They called about siding. Let them feel heard before going anywhere else. Two to three minutes. No more. This earns you the right to pivot.

Say something like

"Everything we discussed about the board-and-batten direction still stands. I've been thinking about this home and I'm genuinely excited about what it's going to look like when it's done."

2
Step 2 · Pivot
Introduce the inspection — transparently
›

Don't sneak the roof in. Name it directly. Then slow down on the rear slope moss and chimney flashing photos. Let the images land before you editorialize.

Say something like

"Before we get into the siding options, I want to walk you through what I documented. It shapes how we should sequence this and I'd rather be upfront about it now."

3
Step 3 · Sequence
Make the roof-first case — honestly
›

This is the truth, not a sales tactic, and they'll know the difference. New Hardie siding on a compromised roof puts a $40K investment at risk.

Core argument

"The roof is the first line of defense on everything that comes after it. I can't in good conscience put new siding on a home with these indicators and not tell you what I'm seeing."

Also: the Ambassador discount only applies when roof + siding + gutters are done together. The math works in their favor as one project.

4
Step 4 · Roof Options
Present Windsor first, always
›

Walk Windsor fully as the orientation point. Then call Vista "what most long-term owners choose." Acknowledge Highlander as valid — just not right for this project.

On the Highlander

"Solid shingle, right for other projects. Given the direction you're going with the exterior, this isn't the one I'd recommend here."

See the Numbers tab for all pricing and financing breakdowns.

5
Step 5 · Return
Come back to siding as the reward
›

This is where their emotional energy lives. Return to it fully. Board-and-batten, trim refinement, front door, gutters. The complete exterior transformation.

Identity anchor

"What you're building is a home that looks like it was designed, not updated. Someone driving down King Drive is going to slow down. Everything we're discussing serves that result."

6
Step 6 · Ambassador
Explain the program — after trust is built
›

Don't lead with this. It lands harder after they already trust you. Frame it as an invitation, not a deal being offered.

Opening frame

"Instead of spending our marketing budget on ads, we invest it in clients who share our vision. The Ambassador program is how we do that — and it's reserved for full exterior projects like this one."

See the Ambassador tab for the full breakdown of what's asked in return.

7
Step 7 · Exit
Leave the room clean
›

No pressure. No lingering. Give them space, anchor the timeline naturally, then go. Leave the estimates. Leave the inspection report.

Exit line — say this exactly

"The number that matters most is the combined package after the Ambassador discount. Everything else is detail. Sleep on whether Windsor or Vista is the right roof — but I wouldn't sleep too long on whether the roof comes first. I think you already know the answer to that."

Inspection — Key Callouts
🌿 Rear Slope
Substantial moss growth — prolonged moisture exposure. Visible oxidation consistent with aging. Roof is in the back half of its service life.
🏚 Chimney Flashing
Previously redone due to improper installation and leakage. History reinforces the need for a full review before investing in siding.
🪟 Windows — Left Elevation
Both second-story windows have failed seals. Double-pane, colonial grids. Flag for evaluation — IGU replacement or full unit.
💨 Ventilation
Gable vents both sides. Configuration may reduce airflow efficiency. Shingle longevity tied directly to attic ventilation — worth a dedicated inspection.
