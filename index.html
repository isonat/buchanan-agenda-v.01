Step 1 · Open
Start on their vision, not yours
›

They called about siding. Let them feel heard before going anywhere else — two to three minutes. This earns you the right to pivot.

Say something like

“Everything we discussed about the board-and-batten direction still stands. I’ve been thinking about this home and I’m genuinely excited about what it’s going to look like when it’s done.”

2
Step 2 · Pivot
Introduce the inspection — transparently
›

Don’t sneak the roof in. Name it directly. Slow down on the rear slope moss and chimney flashing photos — let the images land before you editorialize.

Say something like

“Before we get into the siding options, I want to walk you through what I documented. It shapes how we should sequence this and I’d rather be upfront about it now.”

3
Step 3 · Sequence
Make the roof-first case — honestly
›

This is the truth, not a tactic. New Hardie on a compromised roof puts a $40K investment at real risk. Ambassador discount only applies when roof + siding + gutters are contracted together.

Core argument

“The roof is the first line of defense on everything that comes after it. I can’t in good conscience put new siding on a home with these indicators and not tell you what I’m seeing.”

If they say: “The roof isn’t leaking right now...”
›
“It’s not leaking today, you’re right. But moss growth at that level means moisture is already living in the system. The chimney flashing has already failed once. What I’m asking you to think about is whether you want to find that out after we’ve put $40,000 of new siding on the house.”
Use inspection photos — let them do the talking
4
Step 4 · Roof Options
Present Windsor first, always
›

Walk Windsor fully. Vista is the alternative for budget-conscious owners. Acknowledge Highlander as valid for other projects — not this one. Ambassador discount requires the full package; roof alone does not qualify.

On the Highlander

“Solid shingle, right for other projects. Given the direction you’re going with the exterior, this isn’t the one I’d recommend here.”

If they ask why gutters are required for the discount...
›
“The gutters are part of the roofline system. When we’re already addressing the roof and fascia, that’s the natural time to do them — crew is already there. The Ambassador program is built around full exterior transformations.”
Complete system, not a bundle deal
If they want to get other bids...
›
“That’s reasonable on a project this size. When you’re comparing, ask every contractor where their number comes from — labor rate, material spec, or warranty depth. On Hardie or Malarkey jobs, those three account for almost all price variation.”
Hold your price. Never discount to compete.
5
Step 5 · Return + Design
Come back to siding — lock in the details
›

This is where their energy lives. Return to it fully. This is also the right moment to confirm design choices while they’re excited, before numbers come out.

Identity anchor

“What you’re building is a home that looks like it was designed, not updated. Someone driving down King Drive is going to slow down. Let me make sure what we have priced actually matches what you’re picturing.”

Design confirms — check before moving to numbers:

Quick Design Confirms
Color — ColorPlus factory or field-painted?
Texture — smooth, Sierra 8, or other?
Trim color — matching or contrasting?
Outside corners — 1×4+1×6 (default) or wider?
Band board / frieze — confirm 1×8?
Shutters — keep, remove, or replace?
If brickwork comes up...
›
“Masonry isn’t something we do — we stay in our lane so we can stand behind what we do. But I have a mason I trust. Happy to make that introduction and recommend sequencing it alongside the exterior work.”
Referral ready — connect, don’t overpromise
6
Step 6 · Ambassador
Explain the program — after trust is built
›

Frame it as an invitation, not a deal. Explicit: requires roof + siding + gutters together. Door is not included today.

Opening frame

“The Ambassador program is reserved for full exterior packages — roof, siding, and gutters together. This project qualifies. Not every project does.”

On the door: “The door isn’t in the Ambassador package today. Once the exterior’s done and you’re ready for it, there’s no reason we can’t extend that relationship.”

If sticker shock comes up...
›
“I completely understand — you came to talk about siding and I’ve put a roof in front of you too. The question isn’t whether to do the roof — it’s whether to do it on your timeline or the roof’s.”
Bridge to Options tab for financing
7
Step 7 · Exit
Leave the room clean
›

No pressure. No lingering. Anchor the timeline, then go. Leave estimates and inspection report.

Exit line — say this exactly

“The number that matters most is the combined package after the Ambassador discount. Sleep on whether Windsor or Vista is the right roof — but I wouldn’t sleep too long on whether the roof comes first. I think you already know the answer to that.”

If they say: “We need to think it over...”
›
“That’s completely fair. The one thing I’d ask you to keep in mind is the sequencing — that determines your timeline. If summer completion is the goal, the material window is closer than it feels. I’ll follow up Tuesday — does morning or afternoon work better?”
Always leave with a specific follow-up day
